Privacy Policy - Contera

Last updated: April 25, 2025

1. Introduction

Contera ("we", "our", "us", or "company") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of users of the Contera platform ("Platform", "Service", or "Solution"). This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, share, and protect information obtained through our Solution, in compliance with the Brazilian General Data Protection Law (LGPD - Law No. 13,709/2018), the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other applicable legislation.

We recommend carefully reading this Policy to understand our practices regarding your data and how we will process it.

2. Definitions

For the purposes of this Privacy Policy, the following terms are defined as:

Personal Data: information related to an identified or identifiable natural person. Metadata: data about other data, which provides information about the characteristics of the referenced data, without including its content. Processing: any operation performed with personal data, including collection, storage, classification, processing, reproduction, transmission, among others. Controller: natural person or legal entity that makes decisions regarding the processing of personal data. User: natural person who uses our Platform. Data Subject: natural person to whom the personal data being processed refers.

3. Data collection

3.1 Data we collect

Contera collects only metadata related to the following sources, without access to the content of files or messages:

Calendar: calendar metadata, including event title, start/end date/time, participants, and description. All calendar data is collected, but each user views only their own data.

Document management: document metadata, including file name, file title, type, creation/modification/viewing date, author, and creation/modification/viewing author. We do not collect folder data or hierarchies. The data is aggregated by user, ensuring that only the user has access to their information.

Voice messages: we process voice messages through transcription and keyword analysis to extract metadata. The extracted metadata includes date, time, activity, duration, client, and user who performed the activity. The privacy of the message content is guaranteed, as we do not access the content of documents, only the metadata.

Email: email metadata, including sender, recipient, subject, date/time, and editing time. We do not collect or access attachment data. The data is aggregated by user, ensuring that only the user has access to their information.

3.2 How we collect

Data is collected through:

  • Authorized connections via OAuth with Google and Microsoft.
  • Access to official APIs of the mentioned platforms, after express user consent.
  • Messages voluntarily sent by users.

3.3 Legal bases for processing

Data processing is carried out based on the following legal grounds:

  • Consent of the data subject.
  • Execution of a contract or preliminary procedures related to a contract.
  • Legitimate interest of the controller, provided that the fundamental rights of the data subject are respected.

4. Use of data

4.1 Processing purposes

The collected data is used for:

  • Generation of pre-filled timesheet based on detected activities.
  • Productivity and time usage analysis (analytics).
  • Continuous improvement of the Platform and its features.
  • Technical support and user service.
  • Fraud prevention and Platform security.

4.2 Analytics

We use the collected metadata to generate statistics and analyses on:

  • Number of tasks performed.
  • Average execution time of a task.
  • Identification of users who performed a specific task.
  • Date and time of task execution.
  • Other productivity and efficiency metrics.

Analytics data is anonymized by company, allowing managers to review their employees' data, while employees with usage access view only their own data. The metrics used for productivity analysis include the activity performed and the execution time per employee. The analysis results are presented through online dashboards or data export to Excel or similar formats.

4.3 Google Workspace API Data Usage and AI/ML Models

When accessing data through Google Workspace APIs, we adhere to strict data usage policies:

  1. Personalized AI/ML Models Only: Any Google Workspace data accessed through Google Workspace APIs is used solely for training personalized AI/ML models that benefit you directly. These personalized models are specific to your account and are designed to enhance your experience with our Platform.
  2. No Use for Generalized AI/ML Models: We explicitly affirm that Google Workspace APIs and any data accessed through these APIs are not used to develop, improve, or train generalized/non-personalized AI and/or ML models.
  3. No Third-Party Transfers for AI/ML: Our application does not transfer any Google user data to third-party AI tools for the purpose of developing, improving, or training generalized/non-personalized AI/ML models.

This approach aligns with our commitment to data minimization and purpose limitation, ensuring that your Google Workspace data is used only for the specified purposes and with appropriate safeguards.

5. Data sharing

5.1 Sharing circumstances

We share data only under the following circumstances:

  • With managers and decision-makers of the same company as the user, as authorized by the organization.
  • With sub-processors necessary for the operation of the Platform.
  • When required by law or court order.

5.2 Sub-processors

We use the following sub-processors to operate our Solution:

Cloud infrastructure: Google Cloud Platform, Amazon Web Services, and Microsoft Azure. Artificial intelligence: Gemini and LLAMA. Payment processing: Stripe.

All sub-processors are subject to data protection contracts that ensure an adequate level of security and confidentiality.

5.3 Data segregation

Our platform is multi-tenant but implements rigorous segregation controls. Data is not shared between:

  • Employees of the same company (unless expressly authorized).
  • Employees of different companies.
  • Between client organizations.

6. International data transfer

6.1 Destination countries

The collected data may be stored and processed on servers located in the following territories:

  • Brazil
  • United States
  • European Union

6.2 Safeguards

To ensure the protection of internationally transferred data, we adopt the following safeguards:

  • Standard contractual clauses approved by data protection authorities.
  • Impact assessment on data protection of receiving countries.
  • Adoption of technical and organizational measures to ensure an adequate level of protection.

7. Data retention and deletion

7.1 Retention period

Data is kept for a period of 5 (five) years after collection, due to the extended limitation period for attorney fees, as provided in Article 85 §14 of the Brazilian Civil Procedure Code. This retention period aims to protect the right to charge and execute these values. There are no different retention periods for different types of data or purposes.

7.2 Deletion procedure

Users can request the deletion of their data at any time through the "Data Deletion" section available on the Platform. After the request, all metadata will be permanently deleted, while statistical data will be maintained. We do not retain data after a deletion request. The confirmation process for data deletion is done online, through request and two-factor authentication. After deletion, we send a confirmation email to the user.

8. Data security

8.1 Technical and organizational measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data, including:

  • Authentication via OAuth2 with Google and Microsoft.
  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest.
  • Role-based access controls.
  • Continuous security monitoring.
  • Regular security audits.

8.2 Compliance with security standards

Our Platform was developed and is maintained according to the following standards and certifications:

  • ISO 27001 (Information Security Management System).
  • SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2).

8.3 Incident response

In case of a security incident that may entail relevant risk or damage to data subjects, we will take the following measures:

  • Notification to the national data protection authority and affected data subjects within a reasonable timeframe.
  • Immediate implementation of containment and remediation measures.
  • Investigation of the incident and adoption of measures to prevent recurrence.

9. Rights of data subject

Data subjects have the following rights:

Confirmation and access: to know if their data is being processed and to access it. Correction: to request the correction of incomplete, inaccurate, or outdated data. Anonymization, blocking, or deletion: of unnecessary or excessive data, or data processed in non-compliance with legislation. Portability: to request the portability of data to another service provider. Information: to be informed about the public and private entities with which their data is shared. Consent revocation: to revoke consent at any time.

To exercise these rights, the data subject must access the "Your Rights" section on the Platform or contact us via email: contera@contera.

10. Changes to the privacy policy

This Privacy Policy will be reviewed annually or whenever necessary to reflect changes in our data processing practices or applicable legislation. Changes will take effect after publication on the Platform. We will notify users about significant changes via email and announcements on the platform or application.
